Computer Misuse Act (1990)

The computer misuse act 1990 is a law and regulation that regulates and criminalises any unauthorised access and modification of computers without the 3rd parties consent. This law also criminalises damage to any computer system and data.

Purpose

The purpose of the regulation is to protect the security of computer systems and data, this is to ensure that data does not get stolen and then used for something it should not be. This regulation also helps to stop cyber attacks from occurring. This is because it gives companies / organizations ways to defend themselves against cyber attacks and there also harsh penalties which will make people think twice in committing the crime. This also gives the police ways to prosecute cyber criminals when they attack organizations, which allows the criminals to be prosecuted more easily.

Review of the Computer Misuse Act

In May 2021 the home secretary announced a review for the computer misuse act, this review was completed in 2023, this review involved getting opinions from stakeholders and the public. The review was based around considering if the computer misuse act was fit for purpose because of technological advances. The review also considered if the police had the required powers to investigate and prosecute cyber criminals.

Examples of Computer Misuse:

  • Accessing a friends account without permission
  • Hacking into a network
  • Downloading software that allows you to bypass logins
  • Using 3rd party software to knock someone offline when playing a video game.

Penalty for Computer Misuse

  • Unauthorised access to computer material – up to six months in prison with a chance of up to a £5000 fine.
  • Unauthorised access to computer materials with intent to commit a further crime – up to a 5-year prison sentence with a chance of an unlimited fine (the fine can be as high or as low as it needs to be)
  • Unauthorised modification of data – up to a 5-year prison sentence with a chance of an unlimited fine.
  • Making, supplying or obtaining anything which can be used in computer misuse offences – up to a 10-year prison sentence with a chance of an unlimited fine.

Real Life Example

An example of this law being broken is two men being arrested for a plan to hack Microsoft’s network. These 2 men was arrested due to repeated attempts infiltrating Microsoft’s network and when they got arrested, they were both charged under the Computer Misuse Act. It was mentioned that they are part of a larger group but at the time there was no additional arrests made.
